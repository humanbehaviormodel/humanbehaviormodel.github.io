Overview

In future road networks, automated vehicles must understand how humans drive, anticipate intentions in mixed traffic, and respond naturally. They must also interpret human instructions (spoken words, gestures, traffic rules) and adapt behavior accordingly.

We are especially interested in approaches that generate realistic and diverse behaviors, quantify uncertainty and risk, and support analysis of safety-critical and rare situations—toward more reliable autonomous driving in complex real-world environments.

Abstract

This workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to discuss next-generation human behavior modeling for autonomous driving. Topics include behavior modeling and prediction of drivers and vulnerable road users, behavior-based simulation and digital twins, evaluation of behavior realism, and human–AV interaction in real traffic.

Our goal is to identify open challenges, useful data sources, and promising research directions that can lead to human-centered behavior models and more reliable autonomous driving in complex real-world environments.
